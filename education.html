Vaccine Education Center

Learn everything you need to know about vaccines, their importance, and how they protect your child's health

Vaccine Basics

What Are Vaccines?

Vaccines are biological preparations that help your body develop immunity to specific diseases. They contain weakened or inactive parts of pathogens that trigger an immune response.

How Do Vaccines Work?

Vaccines train your immune system to recognize and fight diseases. They help your body create antibodies and memory cells without causing the actual disease.

Herd Immunity

When enough people in a community are vaccinated, it creates protection for everyone, including those who cannot be vaccinated due to medical reasons.

Vaccine Safety

Safety Testing

Vaccines undergo rigorous testing in multiple phases before approval. They are continuously monitored for safety even after approval.

  • Pre-clinical laboratory testing
  • Phase I, II, and III clinical trials
  • FDA review and approval
  • Ongoing safety monitoring

Side Effects

Most vaccine side effects are mild and temporary. Serious adverse reactions are extremely rare.

Common (Mild)

  • Soreness at injection site
  • Low-grade fever
  • Mild fussiness

Rare (Serious)

  • Severe allergic reaction
  • High fever (over 104°F)
  • Prolonged crying

Vaccination Schedule

Why Follow the Recommended Schedule?

Optimal Timing

Vaccines are scheduled when your child's immune system can best respond and when they're most vulnerable to diseases.

Early Protection

Starting vaccines early protects infants when they're most vulnerable to serious complications from diseases.

Complete Immunity

Following the full schedule ensures your child develops complete, long-lasting immunity.

Diseases Prevented by Vaccines

Serious Diseases

Measles

Highly Contagious

Can cause brain damage, pneumonia, and death. Before vaccines, killed 2.6 million children annually worldwide.

Polio

Paralysis

Can cause permanent paralysis and death. Thanks to vaccines, it's been eliminated from most countries.

Whooping Cough

Dangerous for Infants

Can cause severe breathing problems, especially in babies under 6 months old.

Success Stories

Smallpox

Completely eradicated worldwide through vaccination - the first disease ever eliminated by vaccines.

Measles Deaths

Decreased by 73% between 2000-2018 due to vaccination programs, preventing over 21 million deaths.

Polio

Cases reduced by over 99% since 1988, from 350,000 to just a few hundred cases per year.

Myths vs Facts

MYTH

"Vaccines cause autism"

FACT

Multiple large-scale studies involving millions of children have found no link between vaccines and autism. The original study making this claim was fraudulent and has been retracted.

MYTH

"Natural immunity is better than vaccine immunity"

FACT

While natural infection can provide immunity, it comes with serious risks including death, disability, and complications. Vaccines provide immunity without these risks.

MYTH

"Too many vaccines overwhelm the immune system"

FACT

A child's immune system can handle thousands of vaccines at once. The current vaccine schedule uses only a tiny fraction of the immune system's capacity.
